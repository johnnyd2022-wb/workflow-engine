NZ Gin Distillery — Licensing Guide

Prepared 30 June 2026 · Fact-checked 2 July 2026 Sale and Supply of Alcohol Act 2012 Hutt City District Licensing Committee

Key finding

To sell gin online directly to the public, you need an off-licence with a Section 40 remote seller endorsement under the Sale and Supply of Alcohol Act 2012. This is applied for through Hutt City Council's District Licensing Committee (DLC) — not ARLA, which is an appellate body only. A real-world precedent from November 2025 (Harrier Springs Distillery) confirms distilleries successfully obtain this licence with sales permitted at any time on any day and delivery hours set by condition (e.g. 7 am–7 pm, Mon–Sun). Your existing customs controlled area licence is relevant background but is separate from the off-licence application.

Your action steps

  • 1
    Contact Hutt City Council's licensing team Email sol@huttcity.govt.nz to confirm the application form, current fee schedule (fees changed July 2025 under the Alcohol Fees Bylaw 2024), and what supporting documents the Hutt City DLC requires for a remote seller application.
  • 2
    Apply for an off-licence with a Section 40 remote seller endorsement This is a single application to the DLC — not a separate endorsement added later. The licence covers online sales of your distilled spirits. Your physical distillery address is the premise for the application, even though sales are remote.
  • 3
    Prepare for the 25-working-day public notification period Applications are publicly notified; any affected party may object under sections 105(1) and 131 of the Act. The DLC hears the application and sets licence conditions including delivery hours.
  • 4
    Build your host responsibility policy and age verification process Age verification prompts are required once at site entry and again immediately before checkout, plus an age check at delivery. Receipts and catalogues must display the licensee name, licence number, and expiry; your website must display an image of (or link to an image of) the licence itself. Delivery providers must participate in your age-check process.
  • 5
    Check whether the Hutt City Local Alcohol Policy 2024 affects your application Adopted October 2024, it may restrict off-licence trading hours (potentially 7 am–10 pm) and could limit further licences in parts of the district. Confirm directly with the DLC whether remote sellers are treated differently from walk-in off-licences under the LAP.

Standard conditions on remote seller off-licences include: no delivery between 11 pm–6 am; no delivery on Good Friday, Easter Sunday, Christmas Day, or before 1 pm on Anzac Day; age verification prompts at site entry and again at checkout (plus age check at delivery); licensee name, licence number and expiry on receipts/catalogues and an image of the licence displayed on the website; no advertised discounts of 25% or more (note s 237 carves out a s 40 holder's own catalogue/price-list).

Your starting point

You have your LCQ (Licence Controller Qualification) — unit standards 4646 and 16705 are done. This means you've cleared the biggest study hurdle. What remains is gathering documents, submitting to Hutt City DLC, passing the Inspector interview, and waiting for the DLC decision. You do not need to redo the LCQ — it's a one-time qualification that stays with you. And the certificate is genuinely needed: a s 40 remote-seller licence must be exercised through appointed certified manager(s) (Subpart 7 of Part 2), though under s 215 the manager need not be on duty at all times.

Step-by-step process

✓

LCQ complete Done

Unit standards 4646 (Act knowledge) and 16705 (host responsibility) are completed. Gather your LCQ certificate or confirmation from ServiceIQ — you'll need this as a supporting document.

2

Line up the licensed-premises link — lodge both applications together Action needed

DLCs expect applicants to be working (or about to work) in licensed premises. Note: a customs controlled area licence is a Customs & Excise Act licence — it is not "licensed premises" under the Sale and Supply of Alcohol Act until the off-licence is granted. The standard owner-operator route: lodge the off-licence (s 40) application and the manager's certificate application together, naming yourself as the proposed appointed manager (a s 40 licence must be exercised through appointed manager(s), though under s 215 they need not be on duty at all times). Confirm sequencing with Hutt City DLC.

3

Gather supporting documents Action needed

Collect: photo ID (passport or NZ Driver's Licence), LCQ certificate, employment letter confirming your role at the licensed premises, and a character reference. Some councils also ask for a CV — confirm with Hutt City DLC at sol@huttcity.govt.nz.

4

Submit application to Hutt City DLC Action needed

Complete the "Application for a Manager's Certificate" form from Hutt City Council. Submit by email to sol@huttcity.govt.nz with your documents and the application fee of ~$316.25 (incl. GST) — confirm current amount as fees changed July 2025.

5

Police and Inspector review ~3 weeks

Your application is forwarded to NZ Police (up to 15 working days to respond; if no response, they're deemed to have no opposition) and to the Licensing Inspector who conducts their own suitability assessment.

6

Inspector interview Up to 1 hour

The Licensing Inspector conducts an interview — typically online via MS Teams, occasionally in-person. Scenario-based questions covering your knowledge of the Act and suitability to manage a licensed premises. See the interview section below.

7

DLC decision ~2 weeks after interview

The Inspector reports to the DLC within 2 weeks of your interview. If unopposed: the DLC approves without a hearing and issues the certificate by post. If there is opposition: the DLC schedules a public hearing.

8

Certificate issued

Your Manager's Certificate arrives by post. Valid for 12 months initially, then renewable for up to 3 years at a time. Renewals consider your ongoing involvement with licensed premises. Once certified, appointments (and changes) of managers must be notified to the DLC and Police within 2 working days. Total timeline: 6–8 weeks if unopposed.

The Inspector interview

Duration

Up to 1 hour

Format

Online (MS Teams) or in-person

Style

Scenario-based — practical situations, not a written test

Legal basis

Section 222, Sale and Supply of Alcohol Act 2012

The Inspector assesses your knowledge of the Act and its purposes, suitability to manage a licensed premises, and practical responses to real situations. They're not trying to trick you — they want to know you can handle the common scenarios a manager faces.

Common interview scenarios

  • A customer appears intoxicated and asks for another drink. What do you do?

    Politely refuse service. You cannot supply alcohol to someone who is, or appears to be, intoxicated. Offer water or food instead. If they become disorderly, you have authority to ask them to leave. Document the incident. You're personally liable if you serve them.

  • Someone who looks under 25 tries to purchase alcohol without ID. What do you do?

    Refuse sale. The challenge-25 principle means you ask for ID if there's any doubt. Only valid IDs are accepted: a current passport (NZ or overseas), NZ Driver's Licence, Kiwi Access Card, or Hospitality NZ 18+ Card. If they can't produce one of these, no sale. No exceptions.

  • A staff member serves an intoxicated person while you're on duty. What happens?

    Both you and the staff member may be liable. As the on-duty manager you're responsible for your team's compliance. Address it immediately — stop further service to that person. Retrain the staff member. Review your host responsibility procedures. Document everything.

  • Someone asks for a drink 5 minutes after your licensed hours end. What do you do?

    Refuse. Trading hours are a licence condition — serving outside them is an offence regardless of circumstances. The customer already having paid doesn't change this. Politely explain the premises' closing time.

  • A minor enters a restricted area of the premises. What do you do?

    Ask them to leave the restricted area immediately. Minors are prohibited from restricted areas (where the principal purpose is the sale or supply of alcohol). They may be in supervised areas if accompanied by a parent/guardian.

  • How do you implement your host responsibility policy for an online/remote sale?

    Age verification at point of sale (date of birth declaration plus ID upload or check-at-delivery). Delivery must include an age check — driver confirms recipient is 18+. No delivery to intoxicated persons. Display licence details on website. No delivery between 11 pm–6 am. Maintain records of refusals.
