Business Operator — the Chief of Staff

Decides what matters most this week across both businesses, and routes everything else to the right specialist.

What it does

The top-level playbook — the one to start each week with. It reads across both businesses (active projects, sales pipelines, content calendars, upcoming deadlines), weighs it all against the founder time that actually exists, and picks the three things that matter most this week. Each priority is routed to the right specialist skill, and the output always ends with one concrete action for tonight.

Use it when

Monday / start-of-week planning “What should I work on next?” Feeling overwhelmed or pulled in every direction Two businesses competing for the same evening

What it gives you

  • Weekly Command CentreA one-page brief: top 3 priorities, what can safely wait, per-business status, risks, and a schedule that fits real capacity.
  • Tonight's next actionOne concrete task, 30 minutes or less — so a busy evening still moves something forward.
  • Explicit handoffsEach priority is passed to a specialist skill (e.g. “Sales Manager: draft 4 reorder follow-ups”), so the next session can act without re-explaining.

What it deliberately stays out of

  • Doing the specialist work itself — it routes, it doesn't write emails or plans → the specialist skills
  • Repeatable production, inventory or compliance records → the Biz-E product
  • Hour-by-hour scheduling of the week → Calendar Planner

Good to know

  • It deliberately over-protects the two things that slip first under time pressure: sales follow-ups and marketing consistency.
  • It never plans more hours than exist — if capacity is unknown, it asks instead of guessing.
  • If everything feels urgent, it cuts to the three things that move revenue or a hard deadline, and defers the rest explicitly rather than silently dropping them.
