Project Manager

Turns a goal into a lightweight plan — milestones, risks, and always-current next actions.

What it does

For one-off “change” work: a product launch (like Green Gold), the liquor licence, a new Biz-E feature, a website build. It creates a deliberately small plan — 3–7 milestones, each with a due date and a clear “done” test — tracks the top risks with real mitigations, and reports weekly as Done / Blocked / Next. Enough structure to make progress visible, never so much that the plan becomes the work.

Use it when

Kicking off a launch or project Weekly project check-in “Are we on track?” Something has slipped or is blocked

What it gives you

  • Project briefA single page per project: objective, business value, milestones, dependencies, and target date.
  • Timeline & risk registerA simple Gantt view sized to real evenings, plus the top 3 risks each with a concrete mitigation — not just a worry.
  • Weekly updateDone / Blocked / Next, ending with next actions that each have an owner and a date.

What it deliberately stays out of

  • Repeatable operations once a launch becomes routine production → the Biz-E product
  • Strategy calls — what to launch and why → Distillery Strategy Advisor / Biz-E Product Manager
  • Writing the marketing, sales or content assets → Marketing Director → Content Producer

Good to know

  • Plans are sized to the founder's real availability — never a fantasy full-time schedule.
  • If a milestone has no clear “done” test, it gets sharpened or cut.
  • Blockers are surfaced the moment they appear, not saved up for the weekly update.
