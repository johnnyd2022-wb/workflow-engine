Founder Ops · Whistlebird + Biz-E

The Skill Library — how we run two businesses on founder time

16 playbooks Claude can act as — a chief of staff, a sales manager, a CTO — each producing concrete, actionable output instead of generic advice.

The idea in one box

Each “skill” is a written playbook that tells Claude how to act as one specific role. Ask it to act as the Sales Manager and it reads that playbook plus the shared business facts, reviews the live pipeline, and hands back drafted follow-up emails — not tips about selling. The organising principle: projects create systems, operations run systems. This library manages the changing work (launches, campaigns, pipelines, licences, plans); the Biz-E product itself runs the repeatable manufacturing operations.

The weekly rhythm

  • 1 · Business OperatorStart of week: pick the top 3 priorities across both businesses and route them.
  • 2 · Sales ManagerDraft this week's follow-ups and reorder nudges — the thing that slips first, protected first.
  • 3 · Marketing Director → Content ProducerOne brief becomes a week of ready-to-post content.
  • 4 · Calendar PlannerEverything lands on a specific day that actually exists in the week.

Ground rules every skill follows

  • Never invent facts. Numbers, dates and claims come from the shared fact files; anything missing is asked for, not guessed.
  • Protect the recipes. Whistlebird's botanical formulas are never written down, surfaced or inferred.
  • Keep the brands distinct. Whistlebird (warm, craft, local) and Biz-E (direct, pain-led) never blur — even in the same session.
  • End with actions. Every output finishes with concrete next steps — each with an owner and a date.
