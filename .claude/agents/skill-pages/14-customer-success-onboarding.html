Customer Success & Onboarding

Gets a manufacturer from spreadsheets to their first working process — fast — and keeps them succeeding after.

What it does

Makes Biz-E easy to adopt and hard to leave. It captures a prospect's real-world process, identifies the first process to model (the one that relieves the most pain fastest), builds an onboarding plan with a dated first-value milestone, scripts demos around their workflow rather than a generic tour, and runs health checks so at-risk customers are spotted early.

Use it when

A deal is won A demo is booked A support question arrives Periodic customer health check

What it gives you

  • Onboarding planMilestones from signup → first modelled process → live use, with a dated time-to-first-value target.
  • Tailored demo scriptOpens on their sharpest pain (“audit prep shouldn't take 20 hours”), then shows their own process traced end-to-end.
  • Support material & health checksSetup checklists, training notes, FAQs, drafted support replies, and green/amber/red account ratings with reasons.

What it deliberately stays out of

  • Selling, pricing and closing → Sales Manager
  • Deciding what the product does next → Biz-E Product Manager — it feeds adoption friction back as evidence
  • Fixing bugs — it triages and drafts the customer holding reply → engineering (CTO)

Good to know

  • North star: time-to-first-value — one working process beats a complete configuration.
  • It speaks the operator's language (audit stress, spreadsheet chaos), never feature lists.
  • It never promises unshipped features, and feature gaps go back to the Product Manager as discovery evidence.
