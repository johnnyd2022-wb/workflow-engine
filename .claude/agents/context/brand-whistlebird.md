# Whistlebird — Brand Voice Guide

Working guide for all Whistlebird copy. Marked _(proposed)_ where it should be confirmed
with the founder. Refine over time; this is v1.

> Prompt to deepen later: "Review all Whistlebird context and produce a full brand voice
> guide covering tone, values, audience, product claims, phrases to use/avoid, and example
> copy for retail, hospitality, social, and distributors."

## Values _(proposed)_

Craft quality · authenticity · NZ / Wellington provenance · small-batch credibility ·
technical care in production · distinctive flavour · award-winning quality · premium but
accessible · relationship-led · grounded, non-gimmicky storytelling.

## Voice & tone

- Confident but not corporate.
- Craft-led but not pretentious.
- Premium but approachable.
- Founder-led and local.
- Clear about product quality and awards — state them plainly, don't oversell.
- Avoid overblown luxury clichés unless deliberately testing that positioning.

## Audience-aware register

- **Retail / trade:** commercial and confident — margin, proof it sells, awards, easy reorder.
- **Consumers:** warm, sensory, occasion-led — flavour, serves, local craft story.
- **Hospitality:** practical and useful — serves, cocktail compatibility, staff education.

See `audiences.md` for the full breakdown.

## Product claims (safe to use)

- Core gin: 700 ml, ~44% ABV, case of 6.
- **Solstice** — live/in market; juniper-forward, savory.
- **Green Gold** (⚠️ hold announcements until launched): gin liqueur, 500 ml, 25% ABV,
  made with **kawakawa and honey** — the NZ-native-botanical story is the hook.
- **Gold — Super Spirit Awards**; **Bronze — NZ Spirit Awards**.
- Wellington / Lower Hutt craft distillery; small-batch; multi-shot distillation.
- Stocked in ~70 stores including Liquorland; some nationwide distribution.

**Do not claim:** specific botanicals/recipe, health claims, or anything about ABV/awards
that isn't confirmed for the specific product being described. When unsure, ask.

## Phrases to lean on

Craft · small-batch · Wellington/NZ made · award-winning · distinctive · juniper-forward
(Solstice) · kawakawa & honey (Green Gold) · hand-made · local.

## Phrases to avoid

"World's best," empty superlatives, luxury-cliché ("liquid gold," "nectar"), gimmicky puns
that undercut the premium positioning, and anything implying mass production.

## Example copy (starter — adapt, don't ship blind)

**Shelf-talker / retail one-liner:**
> Whistlebird — Wellington small-batch gin. Gold at the Super Spirit Awards. 44%, 700 ml.

**Consumer social (occasion):**
> Award-winning Wellington gin, made in small batches down the road. Pour it long with a
> good tonic and a wedge of citrus. 🌿

**Distributor line:**
> A distinctive, award-winning NZ craft gin with proven retail sell-through and a founder
> who knows the trade. Reliable supply, strong margin, easy reorder.
