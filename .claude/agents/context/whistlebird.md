# Whistlebird — Business Facts

Wellington / Lower Hutt craft gin distillery. Real, trading business with products,
awards, wholesale relationships, and store presence — not idea-stage.

> **Boundary:** never expose or infer secret botanical recipes or exact proprietary
> formulations. Manage process *structure*, milestones, sensory frameworks, costing, and
> launch checklists — not the formula. The founder supplies formula detail only in a
> private setting when explicitly needed.

## Product

- Core product: **gin**, 700 ml bottles, typically **~44% ABV** (earlier refs 43%/45%).
- Case size: **6 bottles**.
- Flavour dose: ~42 ml per 700 ml bottle. Uses multi-shot / concentrated distillation.
- Liqueur format (new): **500 ml bottles** — first release **Green Gold** at **25% ABV**.

## Commercial traction

- ~**70 bottle stores** reached in ~18 months.
- **Q3 2026 target: ~85 stores by end of September** (~1 new store/week while licence
  work owns the evenings).
- Stocked by several local **Liquorland** stores; some nationwide distribution.
- Awards: **Gold — Super Spirit Awards**; **Bronze — NZ Spirit Awards**.

## Economics (verify current figures before quoting externally)

- Latest wholesale price: **~$47.47 + GST** per bottle (earlier refs: $46.25, $49.56 +GST).
- Cost to produce: **~$25.55 per bottle after GST**.
- GST: **15%**. Profit tax assumption: **28%**.
- See `offers-pricing.md` for the worked case/margin economics.

## Sales model (planning assumptions)

- Target: **~4 new stores per week**.
- Initial order: **1 case** (6 bottles) per new store.
- Sell-through: **~2 bottles/week** per store.
- Restock cycle: **~3 weeks**.
- Implication: disciplined follow-up and reorder tracking is the growth engine. See
  `projects/whistlebird/SALES_PIPELINE.md`.

## Products & experiments

- **Core gin** — the commercial product above.
- **Solstice** — **live / in market** (released; has been live for months as of Jul 2026).
  Juniper-forward, savory profile; blue + yellow label.
- **Green Gold** — **about to release** (as of 2026-07-02). Gin liqueur: **25% ABV,
  500 ml**, target shelf price **~$30**. Wildflower base spirit with a kawakawa-and-honey
  simple syrup (sugar, honey, water, kawakawa) — hence "green gold." Status: bottles in
  hand, labels at the printer, batch close to ready. First of a planned **liqueur range**.
- **Liqueur range** — further liqueurs planned to follow Green Gold.
- **Pink gin experiment** — 30 g rhubarb / 700 ml turned pink; 1.5 kg / 36 L did not;
  UV exposure turned pink → orange within a day. Stability/testing is the open risk.
- **Zero% gin / Dry July concept** — hydrosol, vacuum process, filtration, preservation.
  Has regulatory, formulation, shelf-life, branding, and market-testing components.

## Production / distillation (structure only — no recipe)

- Ultra-concentrate approach. Typical lab/small batch: 1.8 L input @ 20% ABV in a glass
  boiling flask → ~1080 ml distillate (incl. hydrosols), treated as multi-shot → diluted
  with ethanol/vodka then to bottling ABV → ~**78 × 700 ml** commercial bottles.
- Scale-up interest: to **10 L input @ 20% ABV**; considering a less-concentrated 3:1
  version; wants more vibrant, sweet, aromatic flavour expression.
- Repeatable production, inventory, and compliance records belong in **Biz-E**, not here.
