# Biz-E — Objection Handling

Responses stay honest and founder-credible (see `context/brand-bize.md`). Never claim an
unshipped feature — say "in progress." Prices from `context/offers-pricing.md`.

## "We already use spreadsheets / they work fine."
> Spreadsheets work right up until an audit or a staff change. Biz-E keeps the flexibility
> you like but adds inventory-by-stage and traceability, so audit prep becomes a click
> instead of a 20-hour scramble. You can start with one process and keep your sheets for
> the rest.

## "Isn't this just another rigid ERP?"
> The opposite — that's exactly why it was built. ERPs make you bend your process to their
> model, usually with consultants. Biz-E lets you configure *your* process — steps, inputs,
> constraints, inventory stages — yourself, through the UI. No implementation project.

## "It's too expensive." / "$150–200/month is a lot."
> Compare it to the hours lost to audit prep, spreadsheet errors, and reconciliations —
> and to the risk if a key person leaves with the process in their head. Core is
> ~$150+GST/month; Compliant ~$199–200 for full audit-ready reporting. Most of the value
> is time you get back.

## "Implementation sounds like a big effort."
> It isn't a consulting engagement. You model one real process to start, run a batch
> through it, and expand from there. It's designed for founder/operators to set up
> themselves.

## "Does it integrate with our accounting?"
> Xero integration is in progress (OAuth connect, invoices, multi-org tenant selection).
> QuickBooks Online and MYOB are on the radar. Happy to flag you when Xero ships.

## "We're too small for this."
> Small is exactly the fit. Big ERPs are overkill and overpriced for a distillery or
> brewery your size — Biz-E is built for small regulated manufacturers who've outgrown
> spreadsheets but don't want enterprise bloat.

## "How do I know it actually works for a distillery?"
> It's built by someone who runs one. Whistlebird is a real, award-winning NZ distillery —
> Biz-E came out of that operational pain, not a whiteboard.

## "We'll think about it." (stall)
> Fair — what would make this a clear yes or no for you? If it's seeing your own process
> in it, I can set up a short pilot with one of your workflows so you're judging the real
> thing, not a demo.
