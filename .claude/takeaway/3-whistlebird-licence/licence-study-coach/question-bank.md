# Licence Study Question Bank

Syllabus for **licence-study-coach**. Every fact below derives from
`liquor-licence-report.html` (prepared 30 Jun 2026, fact-checked 2 Jul 2026 against
legislation.govt.nz, an issued s 40 licence, and HPA guidance). Items tagged `[verify]`
were low-confidence in the report — teach them as "confirm with official source".

> Not legal advice. If any answer here conflicts with the Act, the HPA Manager's Guide,
> or the DLC, those sources win — flag the conflict, don't drill the error.

## Topic 1 — Object of the Act

- Q: What is the object of the Act, and which section? → **s 4**, two limbs: (a) sale,
  supply, and consumption undertaken safely and responsibly; (b) harm from excessive or
  inappropriate consumption should be minimised. (Common wrong answer: s 5 — the report
  was corrected on exactly this.)
- Q: Why does the inspector care that you know s 4? → Every discretionary decision a
  manager makes should be defensible against the object of the Act.

## Topic 2 — Key sections (sprint list)

- s 4 — object of the Act
- s 40 — remote seller off-licence endorsement
- s 214 — manager on duty whenever alcohol sold/supplied; **s 214(4)** name displayed
- s 215 — relaxes "on duty at all times" for s 40 remote sellers (manager must still be **appointed**)
- s 218 — manager must hold the prescribed qualification (LCQ)
- s 222 — criteria for granting a manager's certificate (the interview's legal basis)
- s 237 — irresponsible promotion, incl. advertising discounts of 25%+ (carve-out: a s 40
  holder's own catalogue/price-list)
- s 239 — must not sell/supply to under-18s
- s 248 — must not sell/supply to intoxicated persons
- s 249 — must not allow people to become intoxicated on premises
- Trap pairs to drill: 237 vs 239 vs 248 vs 249; 214 vs 215 vs 218.

## Topic 3 — Age and ID

- Legal drinking (purchase) age: **18**. Challenge age best practice: **25**.
- The only 4 valid evidence-of-age documents: **any current passport (NZ or overseas)**,
  NZ Driver's Licence (photo), Kiwi Access Card, Hospitality NZ 18+ Card.
- Not valid: overseas driver's licence, student ID, bank cards, loyalty cards, **expired
  anything**. "I forgot it" = refuse, no exceptions.
- Trap: "UK driver's licence?" → invalid. "UK passport?" → valid (current).

## Topic 4 — Intoxication

- Refuse when **2+ signs** present across: appearance (flushed, glassy/red eyes,
  dishevelled), behaviour (aggressive, overly friendly, loud, confused), coordination
  (stumbling, spilling, bumping), speech (slurred, incoherent, repetitive).
- Also refuse if further alcohol **would result in** intoxication — don't wait for signs.
- Serving an intoxicated person: **s 248**, personal liability. Allowing intoxication on
  premises: **s 249**.

## Topic 5 — Manager's on-duty obligations

- Present whenever alcohol is sold/supplied (subject to s 215 for remote sellers);
  name displayed prominently (s 214(4)); staff comply with licence conditions; refuse
  intoxicated/underage; run the host responsibility policy; trade within licensed hours
  only; **record refusals and incidents**.
- Staff member serves an intoxicated person on your shift → **both** of you may be
  liable; stop service, address, retrain, document, review procedures.
- Once certified: manager appointments/changes notified to DLC **and Police within
  2 working days**.

## Topic 6 — Remote seller specifics (his licence — drill hardest)

- Structure: an **off-licence with a s 40 remote-seller endorsement** (single DLC
  application, not a separate licence category).
- Sales permitted **any time, any day**; delivery hours set by condition.
- Delivery prohibited: **11 pm–6 am daily**, and on **Good Friday, Easter Sunday,
  Christmas Day, and before 1 pm Anzac Day**.
- Age verification **twice online** (site entry + immediately before checkout) **and at
  delivery** — driver confirms 18+, refuses delivery to intoxicated recipients, refusals
  recorded.
- Website must display licensee name, licence number, expiry, and an image of (or link
  to an image of) the licence; receipts/catalogues carry name, number, expiry.
- No advertised discounts ≥ 25% (s 237), except within the s 40 holder's own
  catalogue/price-list.
- Host responsibility policy must cover the online purchase flow end to end.
- s 215: appointed manager need not be on duty at all times for a s 40 licence — but a
  manager must be appointed, which is why the certificate is needed at all.

## Topic 7 — Process and numbers

- Application fee: ~$316.25 incl. GST `[verify — Hutt City bylaw, fees changed Jul 2025]`
- Police response window: 15 working days (silence = deemed no opposition)
- Inspector report to DLC: ~2 weeks after interview; total 6–8 weeks unopposed
- Certificate validity: 12 months first issue, then up to 3-year renewals
- Off-licence public notification: 25 working days; objections under ss 105(1) and 131
- National maximum hours: on-licence 8 am–4 am; off-licence 7 am–11 pm
- Hutt City LAP may set off-licence hours 7 am–10 pm `[verify — and whether it applies
  to remote sellers at all]`
- Interview: up to 1 hour, scenario-based, usually MS Teams; legal basis s 222
- A customs controlled area licence is **not** "licensed premises" under this Act.

## Topic 8 — Scenario frameworks (grade against these)

1. **Intoxicated customer wants another drink**: spot 2+ signs → refuse politely → offer
   water/food → ask to leave if disorderly → document → note personal liability (s 248).
2. **Looks under 25, no ID**: refuse → name the 4 valid IDs → no exceptions.
3. **Staff served an intoxicated/underage person on your shift**: stop service now →
   shared liability → retrain → document → review procedures.
4. **Sale requested after licensed hours**: refuse, absolute; already-paid changes
   nothing; explain closing time.
5. **Minor in a restricted area**: remove immediately; supervised areas only with
   parent/guardian.
6. **Host responsibility for a remote sale**: DOB + ID at checkout → age check at
   delivery → no delivery to intoxicated persons → licence details on site → no 11 pm–6 am
   delivery → refusal records.
7. **Suitability opener** (not in the report — practise anyway): why the certificate,
   describe the premises and your role. Answer maps to the s 40 structure: remote-seller
   off-licence must be exercised through an appointed certified manager; you are the
   owner-operator and proposed manager.
